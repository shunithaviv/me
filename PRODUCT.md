# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who land on shunit.ai after meeting Shunit, seeing her work, or following a link: recruiters, collaborators, fellow engineers/researchers, and friends. They arrive curious about who she is and want a quick, memorable read plus a way to reach her work. Short visits, often on mobile.

## Product Purpose

A personal home page for Shunit Haviv Hakimi. This is an intentionally temporary "under construction" version: it should feel alive and characterful rather than a dead placeholder, while the real content is still being written. Success = a visitor smiles, gets who she is at a glance, follows a link, and remembers the site.

## Brand Personality

A blend of three registers held in tension: playful & bold (color, motion, a wink of humor), technical & sharp (precise, engineered, monospace accents, nothing sloppy), and warm & personal (human, approachable, first-person voice). Three words: playful, precise, human. It should read like a person who builds serious things and doesn't take herself too seriously.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev-portfolio templates (dark navy, "Hi, I'm X" + skill bars + timeline).
- Corporate SaaS landing pages (hero-metric template, identical feature-card grids).
- Dead "Coming soon" splash pages with a mail icon and nothing else.
- Neon-on-black "crypto/hacker" cliché that a category-reflex would reach for.

## Design Principles

- **Alive, not parked.** A temporary site can still have a pulse. Motion and personality signal a person is actively here.
- **Show the seams, on purpose.** Lean into the "under construction" honesty as a design device rather than hiding it.
- **Engineered play.** Every playful element is executed with precision. Fun is not an excuse for sloppy craft.
- **First person, few words.** Speak as Shunit, briefly. The visitor's time is short.
- **One memorable move.** Do one distinctive thing well rather than five decorations.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (all motion has a still fallback). WCAG AA contrast in both light and dark. Keyboard-navigable links with visible focus. Video is muted, non-essential, and captioned by context.
